CONSPIRACY, IN THEORY AND PRACTICE

Many people are confident in their place in the world. In a ball of action and reaction (which is debatable, but I'll save that for later) as complex and unpleasant as the world we know and can't escape, nothing can be certain. If thinking about this doesn't make you shiver, then you haven't been paying attention.

The greatest thing about the world is the only thing we can be certain about. DesCartes said it and DesCartes was right (about this, at least): the only thing one is certain about is one's own thoughts. I really like my thoughts, they're the best thing about me. I like my thoughts so much that it doesn't even bother me that my most brilliant thoughts don't get shared with everyone else, so very few people actually realise how smart I am. This leads me to the frightening part; No matter how smart I seem to be, there is always someone smarter than I am. Now, I'm pretty darn clever: I find I'm able to learn to do almost anything, with a few small exceptions, I know a little bit that's relevant to nearly every situation that crops up... blah blah blah... I'm just great. But, still, there are people who are smarter. Add to this equation the fact that given my brains, abilities and destructive inclinations are all sufficient for me to be a danger to society as we know it.

It seems to be human nature to be afraid of people who are smarter than you are. The evidence is everywhere, from the school-yard bully who punches the shit out of the kid who can do math, to the strict controls on intellectuals at universities through grants and ethics committees, to the countless persecutions and attacks upon intelligent people and free thinkers everywhere. This is the greatest conspiracy of all. It reaches every where and everyone is on one side or the other, some changing sides at times, and most don't even realise that it is beneath their feet.

So, now that we have pointed out the global conspiracy to keep down the intelligent, let's flesh it out a bit. This conspiracy is led by stupid people. Somewhere at the middle of this pecking order of evil is the government (I'm lumping all governments together here, for simplicities sake), an archaic dinosaur that can't feel it's own ass. Modern governments are almost universally arbitrary buracracies in place to hold people in their social caste and trade resources to keep them out of the hands of people who can use them effectively. On top of that, governments are run by small minded fucking buffoons; albeit, they are usually charismatic, but this does not make them suitable rulers! My life and the lives of those like me should not be controlled by Stockwell Days, George Bush Jr.s, Mike Harris's, and Jesse (the thought that this man's opinion can actually be taken seriously makes my ears bleed and my eyes go dry with rage (in response to the Colorada shootings a few years ago he said, "It's no wonder our kids are shooting each other if we're telling them that they're glorified monkeys in school." This is as direct a quote as I can give, as it happened a long time ago and I didn't think to record any references. This particular speech was in support of several states making it illegal to teach anything but good old Christian creationism in schools), and he's a fucking US governor!) Venturas. The fact that the system allows people to elect these men and others like them into positions of power is only proof that people are too fucking stupid to be allowed to vote. I can't wait until 2012 when Stone Cold Steve Austin and The Rock determine the identity of the president of the United States with the 2K + 12 Electoral Smack Down! and Stockwell Day, fed up with the insufficiently conservative Canadian still just the Reform Alliance Party and forms the new No Coloureds and No Queers Party (I wrote this well before the whole 'everyone hates Stockwell' fiasco. Oh well, it proves my point, and I'll bet that whoever replaces him will be no better). Where the fuck do we think we're taking ourselves?

A step or two above the government is religion. The inherently bad aspects of religion are obvious to anyone who has managed to read this far. The problems with every religion are discussed ad nauseum everywhere and I don't have the stamina or desire to discuss them now. The problems are there though, and even the religious must set down their little symbols eventually and recognise them, unless they're stupid, in which case they'll go on violating their own rules and trying to find solace on the other side of a quarter until, well, I don't know when it will end, but I wish I could have something to do with it. If you have read this far and you don't see what I'm saying, you either skipped a bunch and should go back, or you are just fooling yourself. Close your eyes and think about it until you cry. I know I did.

To get to the point, religion/conspiracy-wise though, there is potential within religion to do quite a bit of the old citizen screwing. Don't look to the most obvious culprit, the one that every thought-protection-hat-wearing paranoid will leap to, the Christian church (I'm, of course, doing a bit of lumping here. The Christian sects aren't as different as they insist they are, and what differences exist aren't worth killing each other over). The Christian church has had its heyday, and the inquisition was a long time ago. The only real danger from Christianity to the common man is from groups of religious yahoos (I'm talking about the type who burn crosses into lawns and pitch rocks at Jews here, people intelligent enough to read this far probably shouldn't be offended by the term religious yahoo, because it shouldn't apply, whether they are religious or not. If it does apply to you though, you should be fucking ashamed, and there's simply no better way to put it) and politicians, judges and other officials who don't properly understand the meaning of 'Separation of church and state', a phrase that appears both explicitly and implicitly in the constitutions of both Canada and the United States. No, the only conspiracy behind the Christians is the same conspiracy that gives stupid people power.

The so called 'new age' religions have some conspiracy value, but this is mostly a business arrangement type of conspiracy. As much as the world needs to be overtaken by some genuinely peaceful tree huggin' goths, religious enlightenment doesn't come at twenty bucks a pop and one should probably be skeptical of people with names like Ravenwood Silvermoon. Likewise, most of the appearance of eastern religions in North America is designed to siphon funds from the dissaticefied. Infact, the availability of instructional manuals on Buddhism, the pre-packaged Zen rock gardens (I actually have one of these, but it was a gift and I think it looks nice), the expensive meditation retreats and the pricy Feng Shui decorators are all pretty contrary to the DIY tendancies of most of the religions and traditions they claim to have grown from. Aside from the accumulation of currency, there is no real dangerous activity one could accuse these religions of.

Now lets look at a possibly dangerous religious group. The Scientologists! This is not to say that the average Scientologist is a danger; the average Scientologist is frankly not too bright. I don't even want to say that the Scientologists are the only bad ones, they're just a good example. Scientologists have built their little cult up into a sizable religion with the help of Hollywood stars and a careful tailoring of their tenets to appeal to the ego of everyone who can overlook a logic gap the size of a Studebaker. Scientology seems to be designed to make people feel smart, as the name might suggest. They offer their own intelligence test (the 'scientometric test' if you can actually believe it) to anyone who will take it, presumably to single out likely members. The 'problem' with this is that smart people are simply too smart to fall into it. I haven't heard of anyone reknowned for their intelligence and/or wisdom, or even a single member of Mensa (note the separation of the two groups) who is a Scientologist. This, however, works in Scientology's favour, as anyone can tell you that a religion's success (on reflection, I think this applys more to the westen word than to the eastern) relys on building a base of followers that feels that their participation in the said religion makes them better than other people and doesn't think about it too much. Simply put, if you replace 'is really smart' with 'will get into the magical paradise' you get Christianity. The creators of Scientology are either idiots, who think themselves smarter than the people who tell them that they aren't all that bright, or they are genius's, who just may be taking over the world as we speak. It terrifies me to think that some day Alec Baldwin and Keanu Reeves could be revered as men of vision and intelligence for embracing Scientology so early on. Keep in mind, however, that I don't think those nutty scientologists are into targeting individuals for persecution, at least until a few of them read this. I'm onto you, you stupid bastards.

Let's move on to the military. The military is an obvious source of paranoia. They've got more guns than the people do (in a sane world, at least), technology that people aren't allowed to know about, sufficient beuracracy to make the normal government blush, and completely legal and government sanctioned brainwashing camps. Well, it shouldn't take Fox Mulder to find the danger in a gang of homophobic brainwashed soldiers, armed to the teeth with high tech gadgets I probably haven't even imagined yet. To be fair, we are involved in a war, of sorts, but can that really justify everything? They're out there, and that's scary. Aside from the obvious killing and brutal beating, what can they do to us though? Simply put, the army is fast moving and very close mouthed. Aside from a few incidents in the gulf (do you remember that? There was to be a super secret amphibious attack somewhere in the Persian Gulf (give me a break, I was like 10) and the American press was waiting on the shore when the boats landed) the military is always way ahead of the press. Unlike the press, the military also knows that not everything needs to be revealed to the public right away. The problem here is the same problem that goes with censorship and traditional education, and media too, to an extent; the information we recieve is the information that someone else has deemed neccesary and suitable for our consumption! Nobody needs to tell me what I am allowed to know; if the remains of an unidentifiable craft were found in the desert in Arizona, I should know, just like I might like to know who Madonna was marrying were I able to scrounge up the hindquarters of a rat from the dark pits of my empty soul. What our society has decided is appropriate information for the general public is based on some grotesquely arbitrary and nonsensical standard that I don't feel I should be forced to be a part of.


That's all for this installment. Next time I'll rave about media, and secret government intelligence agencies, and law enforcement. Until then, keep your eyes open.
